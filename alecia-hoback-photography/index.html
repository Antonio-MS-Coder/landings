Before the shutter

Three lines make a
workable brief.

A photography inquiry does not need to be long. It needs to be specific. Answer these three and Alecia can tell you quickly whether the date is open and what the work would involve.

01

What needs photographing

The subject, plainly put — people, a place, a product, an occasion. Say how many, and say if there is one shot the whole thing hinges on.

e.g. “Four people, outdoors, about an hour.”

02

When and where

A date, or a window of dates, and the address or general area. Light and travel both depend on it, so an approximate time of day helps more than you would think.

e.g. “A Saturday in the fall, near Smyrna.”

03

Where the images end up

Prints on a wall, a website, a printed program, a phone screen. The destination shapes how a frame is built, so it belongs in the first message rather than the last.

e.g. “One large print, rest online.”

The ground covered

La Vergne, and the
middle of Tennessee.

La Vergne sits on the seam between Nashville and Murfreesboro, with Smyrna and Antioch a few minutes either side. A good deal of Middle Tennessee is a short drive from that corner of the map.

If your date falls somewhere outside that circle, say so in your note rather than assuming the answer. It is a question worth asking.
