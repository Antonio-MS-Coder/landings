A working farm that hosts weddings.

Most of the year, Crafton Farms grows things — strawberries, vegetables, rows you can walk and pick yourself. The rest of the year, the same ground holds a ceremony. The barn does not stop being a barn on your wedding day. That is the whole point of it.

A double-door barn

The listing describes a barn with double doors — the kind that open wide enough to make the entrance part of the ceremony, and wide enough to move tables, staging and equipment without dismantling anything.

An outdoor ceremony area

Listed as a separate outdoor ceremony space, distinct from the barn. Which means the two do not have to compete for the same hours — vows outside, reception in, without a room flip in between.

Open in all four seasons

The Tennessee Agritourism listing shows the farm operating spring, summer, fall and winter. A December date and a June date are both on the table — what changes is what is growing outside the doors.

What is not on this page

No pricing, no package list, no claim about what a booking includes. None of that has been published, so none of it is written here. Ask the farm and you will get the real answer instead of a guess.

Why two squares are blank. An almanac that invents a harvest is not an almanac. Crafton Farms has published strawberries and vegetables; it has not published a fall or winter crop.
